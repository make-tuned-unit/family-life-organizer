Best shared shopping list app (2026): an honest comparison

Whether you want a power tool for cooks or just a fast list the whole family can edit, here is a fair look at the main shared shopping list apps — and where Kinrows fits in.

The short answer: AnyList is best for keen cooks who want recipes and meal planning tied to the list, OurGroceries is the best simple fast shared list, Cozi is best if you want the list inside a wider family organiser, and Google Keep is the quickest free option for Google households. Kinrows — launching fall 2026 — puts shared lists and a pantry view inside a private all-in-one app with an AI concierge that can add items for you.

How we picked

We are Kinrows, so this is an honest comparison from an interested party. Shopping list apps split by how much you want them to do — a quick checklist, or a full grocery-and-recipe system — so we have grouped them that way. And to be clear: Kinrows is new and launches in fall 2026, so for a shared list you can use today, the options below are the right call.

Best for keen cooks: AnyList

AnyList is the most capable option for people who plan meals. Beyond a clean shared grocery list with smart categories, it imports recipes from the web, builds meal plans, and turns recipe ingredients into list items. If cooking and meal planning drive your shopping, AnyList is the power tool — it has a free tier and a paid upgrade for the deeper features.

Best simple shared list: OurGroceries

OurGroceries does one job and does it fast: a shared grocery list that everyone in the family can add to and check off in real time, across phones. It is free with ads, with an optional ad-free upgrade. If you just want a no-fuss list the whole household keeps in sync, this is the cleanest choice.

Best inside a family organiser: Cozi

If you would rather not run a separate app just for groceries, Cozi keeps shared shopping and to-do lists alongside its family calendar and meal planner. It is free with ads. For households that want the list to live next to the schedule, Cozi is the established all-in-one.

Best quick free option: Google Keep

If your family already uses Google, a shared Keep checklist is the fastest way to get a list going — free, instant, and synced across everyone's devices. It is not grocery-specific and has no categories or meal planning, but for a simple shared list with zero setup, it is hard to beat.

Best list inside a private all-in-one app: Kinrows

Kinrows, launching fall 2026, keeps shared lists — including a grocery list that connects to a pantry view so you can see what you already have — inside a whole household app, next to the calendar, budget, and the rest of family life. Two things set it apart: privacy by design, with household data isolated at the database level and no ads, and an optional AI Concierge that can add items to a list when you just ask ("add milk and nappies to the shop"). The honest caveats: it is iPhone-first, not yet released, and a broader household app rather than a dedicated grocery tool — so if you want the deepest recipe features today, AnyList is the better fit.
